Making Wine: Getting Started

Only as fancy as you want it to be

Making your own wine allows you to experiment and design a wine that you and your friends will enjoy.

Start off small by making only a gallon of wine. Then you can increase the quantity as you get more comfortable in the process.

Types of Wine

  • Dry Wine
  • Semi-Sweet Wine
  • Sweet Wine

Getting Started

You don’t need expensive equipment to begin. Use your imagination; a milk crate can de-stem your grapes. Muscle power or body weight with cheese cloth and buckets can be used to squeeze the juice out of the fruit.

The main purchases to considered is a good yeast for the fermentation, along with a stopper with an airlock to allow the gases to escape but not allow outside air in during the fermentation proccess.

Want it dry or sweet?

If you enjoy a drier wine, allow the fermentation process to consume the majority of the natural sugar.

For that semi-sweet to sweet wine. Stop the fermentation before the yeast has consumed more than 80% of the natural sugar. If you are worried about the possibility of the wine being too dry for your taste. Save some grape juice to use on the back end of the fermentation process.

Still questioning whether You Can Do It?

Start with a kit that has all the ingredients you neet. Or if you town has a local Beer and Wine making supply store, see if they have a class to walk you through the proccess.
